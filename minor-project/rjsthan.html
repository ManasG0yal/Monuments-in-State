Rajasthan

Rajasthan is a state in northern India.It covers 342,239 square kilometres (132,139 sq mi) or 10.4 per cent of India's total geographical area. It is the largest Indian state by area and the seventh largest by population. It is on India's northwestern side, where it comprises most of the wide and inhospitable Thar Desert (also known as the Great Indian Desert) and shares a border with the Pakistani provinces of Punjab to the northwest .

3.Jantar Mantar
Another historical site to be explored in the vibrant Pink City in Rajasthan is Jantar Mantar. It is a celestial observatory that was commissioned by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh. It is home to the largest stone sundial in the world, known as Samrat Yatra which makes this place a significant UNESCO World Heritage. It is also one of the archaic Rajasthan historical places.

4.Hawa Mahal
Hawa Mahal is another historical gem located in Jaipur, Rajasthan and is known for rare and vast architecture. Located in the lively capital city, Hawa Mahal is visited by almost all the tourists spending their vacations in the area. It was constructed by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh in the year 1799 and it served as a window for the ladies staying in royal palace.
