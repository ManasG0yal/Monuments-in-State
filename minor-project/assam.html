Assam

Assam (/əˈsæm, æ-/;[10][11] Assamese: [ˈɔxɔm] (listen)) is a state in northeastern India, south of the eastern Himalayas along the Brahmaputra and Barak River valleys. Assam covers an area of 78,438 km2 (30,285 sq mi). The state is bordered by Bhutan and Arunachal Pradesh to the north; Nagaland and Manipur to the east; Meghalaya, Tripura, Mizoram and Bangladesh to the south.

Famous Heritage Places in Assam

  • Rang Ghar
  • Talatal Ghar
  • Surya Pahar
1.Rang Ghar
Rang Ghar is often regarded as the ‘Colosseum of the East’. Being one of the oldest surviving amphitheaters in Asia, Rang Ghar is one of the best Sivasagar historical sites in Assam. Rang Ghar meaning the ‘House of Entertainment’ was originally a two-storied building. It was used as a royal sports pavilion, especially during the Rongali Bihu Festival.

3.Surya Pahar
Owing to many archaeological discoveries, Surya Pahar in Assam is also referred to as an Archaeologist’s Gold Mine.
