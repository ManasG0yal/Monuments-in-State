Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh is a state in central India. Its capital is Bhopal, and the largest city is Indore, with Jabalpur, Ujjain, Gwalior, Satna, and Guna being the other major cities. Madhya Pradesh is the second largest Indian state by area and the fifth largest state by population with over 72 million residents.

Famous Heritage Places in Madhya Pradesh

  • The Great Stupa of Sanchi
  • The Khajuraho Group of Temples
  • Gwalior Fort
  • Omkareshwar Temple
1. The Great Stupa of Sanchi
Sanchi Stupa enjoys extraordinary fame as far as historical places in Madhya Pradesh are concerned. Calling this site ‘historical’ would be an understatement as its construction dates back to as far as the 2nd and 1st century BC.

3.Gwalior Fort
If you find hill forts fascinating, Gwalior Fort will give the history buff in you every reason to fall in love with this structure sitting atop a hill in Gwalior. The inscriptions, the monuments, and the architecture of the fort – each boasts an unprecedented diligence and charm.A visit to the fort will take you back in time when this culturally rich city was ruled by Tomars.

4.Omkareshwar Temple
One of the most prominent historical places in Madhya Pradesh, Omkareshwar Temple finds its name on the bucket list of a lot of culture vultures in India.The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is located on an island called Shivapuri in the river Narmada that resembles the shape of the OM symbol. It is one of the most revered religious sites for the followers of Hinduism as it is one of the 12 Jyotirlinga shrines of Shiva.
